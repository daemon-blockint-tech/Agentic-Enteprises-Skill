## Audience and cadence

| Audience | Cadence | Focus |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **CTO / peer execs** | Monthly | Portfolio, incidents, dependencies |
| **CFO / FP&A** | Monthly + planning | Spend vs plan, commits, capex |
| **Board / audit committee** | Quarterly | Risk, major bets, material variance |
| **SteerCo (transformation)** | Biweekly during peak | Milestones, blockers, decisions needed |

Delegate **slides with data** to FinOps, portfolio leads, and economists; VP owns **story and decisions**.

## Narrative structure

Use this order (one page executive summary first):

1. **Headline** — on track / at risk / off track (one sentence)
2. **Decisions needed** — max 3, with options and recommendation
3. **Metrics** — 5–7 portfolio KPIs vs plan (no service-level dumps)
4. **Risks** — top 5 with owner and mitigation date
5. **Portfolio** — what shipped, what slipped, what we stopped funding
6. **Appendix** — detail for questions only

State **options rejected** and **why** — builds trust with CFO and board.

## CFO and finance

Emphasize:

- **Run-rate trajectory** and drivers (growth, efficiency, one-times)
- **Commit utilization** and true-up exposure
- **Capex vs plan** by program; tie to `director-infrastructure-capex-accounting` narrative
- **Unit economics** link when product shares drivers

Avoid GL jargon — point to accounting partners for policy and close topics.

## Board and audit

Focus on **materiality** and **risk**:

- Major outages (customer impact, systemic fix)
- Regulatory or residency changes
- Multi-year DC or cloud bets — schedule, $ envelope, kill criteria
- Security incidents on infrastructure estate (facts only; no blame)

Do not present unvalidated savings — label estimates and owners.

## SteerCo and QBR

**SteerCo** — decisions, escalations, cross-functional blockers (product, security, finance).

**QBR** — retrospective: goals met, budget variance explained, next-quarter priorities adjusted.

Close every briefing with **named owners and dates** for actions and follow-up metrics.
