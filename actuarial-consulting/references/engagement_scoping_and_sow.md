## Scoping intake

Capture in writing before proposal:

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Who is the **client** and **counterparty** (parent vs sub)? | Contracting entity, data access |
| What **decision** is due and by when? | Drives depth and deliverable format |
| Which **lines** and **measurement bases** (statutory, GAAP, IFRS 17)? | Avoid scope creep |
| Is there an **appointed actuary** or internal signatory? | Defines reliance and review |
| What **prior work** exists (models, memos, DD reports)? | Reuse vs independent review |
| Any **legal hold** or transaction confidentiality? | Data room and distribution |

## SOW structure

Recommended sections:

1. **Background and objectives** — decision supported, not marketing language
2. **Scope of services** — in-scope tasks as verbs (review, validate, opine support, advise)
3. **Out of scope** — explicit exclusions (tax, legal, IT implementation, investment advice)
4. **Deliverables** — title, format, recipient, draft vs final
5. **Client responsibilities** — data, SMEs, workshop attendance
6. **Timeline and dependencies** — data receipt gates
7. **Fees and expenses** — fixed, T&M, cap, invoicing
8. **Confidentiality and data security**
9. **Reliance and limitations** — third parties, public use
10. **Governing law and dispute** — coordinate with `commercial-counsel`

### Deliverable examples (pick applicable)

- Actuarial memorandum (PDF + workbook index)
- Reserve / pricing **review exhibit set**
- Model validation report with finding register
- Management presentation (PPT) and speaker notes
- Data request list and reconciliation memo
- Board or regulatory **briefing pack** (slides + appendix index)

## Data and access

Specify in SOW:

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Data cutoff | Policy/claim bordereaux as-of date |
| Systems | Admin, claims, reinsurance, data warehouse |
| Format | Secure transfer, field dictionary, PII handling |
| Reliance | Client certification vs independent testing |
| Retention | Workpaper archive period |

Flag **incomplete data** as a scope change trigger, not an silent assumption.

## Fees and change control

| Model | When to use |
|---|---|
| Fixed fee | Well-defined DD or validation with clear deliverables |
| Time and materials | Exploratory review with monthly cap |
| Phase-gated | M&A: phase 1 red-flag, phase 2 deep dive |

Change orders for: new LOBs, extended opinion support, additional models, regulator requests outside SOW.

## Reliance and limitations

Standard limitations (adapt with counsel):

- Work for **client management** only unless named reliance parties listed
- Not **legal**, **tax**, or **investment** advice
- Subject to **data accuracy** provided by client
- No guarantee of regulatory acceptance or deal closure
- **Effective date** of analysis; no duty to update unless retained

## Milestone and review plan

Example timeline for reserve review engagement:

| Week | Activity | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Kickoff, data request | Signed data inventory |
| 1–2 | Data QC, reconcile to prior | QC memo |
| 3–4 | Technical analysis (`actuary`) | Draft exhibits |
| 5 | Internal EQ review | Review log |
| 6 | Draft memo to client | Draft v1 |
| 7 | Management readout | Deck + Q&A log |
| 8 | Final memo | Signed deliverable |

Build **buffer** before board, audit, or regulatory dates.
