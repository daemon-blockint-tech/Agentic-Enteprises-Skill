## Req intake checklist

Capture before opening or advertising:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Headcount approval | Budget owner, cost center, req ID |
| Role type | Backfill, net-new, contractor conversion |
| Level / band | Framework level, not only title |
| Location & work model | Onsite, hybrid, remote; visa sponsorship |
| Target start | Hiring urgency vs quality tradeoff |
| HM and interview team | Named owner; backup interviewer |
| Must-haves vs nice-to-haves | Testable skills; max 5–7 must-haves |
| Anti-profile | Levels too junior/senior, industries to avoid |
| Comp direction | Band from `comp-analysis`; approval for exceptions |
| Diversity goals | Slate composition goals per org policy |
| Success at 90 days | Outcomes, not skill laundry list |

## Prioritization framework

When reqs exceed capacity, stack-rank using:

1. **Revenue or delivery risk** — unfilled role blocks roadmap or SLA
2. **Approved start date** — committed customer or launch date
3. **Pass-through health** — reqs with viable pipeline vs zero qualified
4. **HM responsiveness** — slow feedback extends time-to-fill for everyone
5. **Cost of agency** — use agencies for scarce profiles after internal sourcing window

Document deprioritization to HMs and leadership; do not silently stall candidates.

## Job description structure

Recommended external JD sections:

1. **Role summary** (2–3 sentences)—impact and team context
2. **What you'll do**—outcomes and responsibilities
3. **What we're looking for**—must-haves grouped by competency
4. **Nice to have**—clearly labeled optional
5. **How we work**—location, travel, on-call if relevant
6. **Benefits and EVP**—pointer to careers page; pay range if legally required
7. **EEO statement**—use org-approved boilerplate

Avoid:

- "Rockstar/ninja" and gender-coded language
- Degree requirements unless legally or genuinely required
- Years-of-experience proxies that exclude non-traditional paths without reason
- Internal jargon candidates cannot decode

## Leveling and titles

1. Map role to **career framework** level before writing JD
2. Resolve title inflation early (e.g., "Senior" without scope)
3. Align interview bar to **level competencies**, not HM personal preference only
4. Document level decision in req notes for offer stage consistency
5. Escalate dual-level reqs (hire L4 or L5) to leadership before interviewing

## Inclusive and compliant posting habits

- Use **gender-neutral** titles and pronouns in templates
- List **pay range** when required by jurisdiction; avoid "DOE" where prohibited
- State **sponsorship** policy clearly to reduce wasted screens
- Offer **reasonable accommodation** language per org template
- Do not state preferences for age, family status, or national origin
- Coordinate non-discrimination and accessibility statements with HR/legal templates

## Posting and channel plan

| Channel | When to use |
|---|---|
| Careers site | All public reqs; source of truth |
| Employee referral | Always; track referral bonus rules |
| LinkedIn / boards | Broad roles; monitor cost per applicant |
| Communities / events | Niche roles (eng, design, GTM) |
| Agencies/RPO | Scarce profiles after internal window |
| University / intern | Early-career programs only |

Tag every applicant with **source** in ATS for metrics.

## Agency and RPO brief

Provide agencies the same **req brief** as internal team plus:

- Exclusivity or non-exclusivity window
- Fee or project terms (reference procurement, not invent)
- Slate size and turnaround SLA
- Submission format (resume, fit note, comp expectations, notice period)
- **No resume spray**—max submissions per week without quality bar
- Feedback loop: pass/fail reasons within 48–72 hours

Score agencies on quality conversion, not submission volume.
