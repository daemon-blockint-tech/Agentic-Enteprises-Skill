# Talent Acquisition

## When to Use

- Open, prioritize, or close **requisitions** tied to workforce and headcount plans
- Write or refine **job descriptions**, leveling, and role success profiles with hiring managers
- Design or improve **interview loops**, stages, scorecards, and **candidate experience** (coordination—not question banks alone)
- Run **ATS/CRM** pipelines, stage definitions, SLAs, and recruiter–HM operating rhythms
- Coordinate **offers** through approval, negotiation support, and close (not legal offer drafting)
- Report **TA metrics**—time-to-fill, pass-through, source mix, diversity funnel, agency performance
- Manage **agencies/RPO** statements of work, scorecards, and slate quality
- Articulate **employer value proposition** for a role or function in hiring materials
- Hand off hired candidates to **onboarding** with structured start information
- Apply **hiring compliance basics**—EEO awareness, OFCCP-style documentation habits, right-to-work checkpoints (not legal advice)

## When NOT to Use

- Proactive boolean search, talent maps, and outreach sequences only → `talent-sourcer`
- Interview question banks, scorecard rubrics, or calibration session design only → `interview-prep`, `interview-system-designer`
- Compensation benchmarking, bands, or equity modeling → `comp-analysis`
- Legal offer letter text, employment law, or policy interpretation → `draft-offer`, `hr-business-partner`
- Org-wide people strategy, culture programs, or executive workforce planning → `chro-advisor`
- Employee HR onboarding, HRIS provisioning, or offboarding → `people-operations-specialist`, `onboarding`
- Req status tracking without process design → `recruiting-pipeline` (when present)
- Employer brand campaigns outside hiring context → `cmo-advisor`, `brand-strategist`

## Core Workflows

### 1. Workforce planning and req intake

1. Confirm headcount approval, budget, level, location, and target start date
2. Align req to team plan, backfill vs net-new, and priority vs other open roles
3. Capture must-haves, nice-to-haves, and anti-profiles from hiring manager
4. Set success metrics for the hire (90-day outcomes, not only skills list)
5. Open req in ATS with correct job code, cost center, and approval chain

**See `references/talent_acquisition_scope.md` and `references/requisition_and_job_design.md`.**

### 2. Job design and posting

1. Draft JD with inclusive language, level expectations, and realistic requirements
2. Map role to career framework or leveling guide; resolve title/level mismatches early
3. Define comp band directionally with `comp-analysis` if needed; do not invent numbers
4. Build posting pack: internal blurb, external JD, interview team roster, agency brief
5. Publish to careers site, boards, and referral channels; tag sources in ATS

**See `references/requisition_and_job_design.md`.**

### 3. Interview process and candidate experience

1. Design loop: stages, interviewers, competencies, time targets, and debrief cadence
2. Publish **candidate-facing** schedule, prep, and feedback SLAs
3. Coordinate panels, take-home boundaries, and work-sample ethics
4. Run debriefs with evidence-based scorecards; avoid consensus without data
5. Manage rejections and withdrawals with respectful, timely communication

**See `references/interview_process_and_candidate_experience.md`.**

### 4. ATS pipeline and hiring manager partnership

1. Define stages, SLAs, and required fields per stage in ATS/CRM
2. Hold weekly HM sync: pipeline health, blockers, calibration, and slate diversity
3. Escalate aging reqs, low pass-through, or HM unavailability early
4. Document decisions, feedback themes, and process changes in req notes
5. Coordinate agency/RPO slates against the same bar as internal sourcing

**See `references/ats_pipeline_and_hiring_manager_partnership.md`.**

### 5. Offers, close, and onboarding handoff

1. Run pre-close checks: references, background scope, comp approval, level sign-off
2. Coordinate verbal offer, written offer routing through `draft-offer` / HR as required
3. Support negotiation within approved bands; escalate exceptions with business case
4. Manage counter-offers and competing timelines without pressure tactics
5. Hand off to onboarding: start date, equipment, buddy, and day-one agenda

**See `references/offers_onboarding_and_agency_management.md`.**

### 6. Metrics, compliance, and reporting

1. Track time-to-fill, time-in-stage, pass-through, and source effectiveness
2. Monitor diversity funnel by stage; investigate drop-offs without blaming candidates
3. Report agency/RPO performance against SLA and quality scorecard
4. Maintain EEO-appropriate documentation habits per org policy (not legal advice)
5. Retrospect closed reqs: what worked, bottlenecks, and JD/process fixes

**See `references/ta_metrics_compliance_and_reporting.md`.**
