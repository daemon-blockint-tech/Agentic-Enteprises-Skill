## P&L ownership model

Clarify what the country P&L includes:

| Line item | Often in-country | Often allocated from HQ |
|---|---|---|
| Local sales compensation | Yes | — |
| Country marketing spend | Yes | — |
| Local G&A (office, admin) | Yes | — |
| Country leadership payroll | Yes | — |
| Product/engineering R&D | Rarely | Yes |
| Global brand campaigns | Partial | Yes |
| Corporate overhead allocation | — | Yes |
| FX translation | Entity reporting | Treasury policy |

Document **allocation rules** and **transfer pricing** assumptions with finance—do not invent (`cfo-advisor`).

## Planning horizons

| Horizon | Purpose | Owner rhythm |
|---|---|---|
| Annual | Targets, budget, headcount envelope | CM + finance + regional president |
| Quarterly | Operating plan refresh, initiatives | CM QBR |
| Monthly | Forecast, hiring, spend control | CM + functional leads |
| Weekly | Pipeline, incidents, hiring | Sales/CS leads |

Align country plan to **global strategic pillars** without duplicating enterprise portfolio work (`enterprise-strategist`).

## Building the country business plan

Minimum sections:

1. **Executive summary** — thesis, targets, top 3 bets, top 3 risks
2. **Market context** — size, growth, share ambition, competition
3. **Revenue plan** — by segment/product/channel with drivers
4. **Cost plan** — fixed vs variable; hiring by quarter
5. **Investment cases** — entry, marketing, systems, partner MDF
6. **Cash and capital** — working capital, capex, HQ funding ask
7. **Initiative roadmap** — outcomes, owners, dependencies on HQ
8. **Risk register** — regulatory, FX, talent, partner, product
9. **KPI tree** — links country metrics to regional/global scorecard

## Revenue and pipeline mechanics

Define **leading indicators** alongside lagging revenue:

| Metric | Use |
|---|---|
| Pipeline coverage (x quota) | Quarter risk |
| Win rate by segment | Positioning / product fit |
| Sales cycle length | Localization / trust |
| ASP / ARPU | Pricing and mix |
| Churn / NRR (if applicable) | CS and product fit |
| Partner-sourced % | Channel health |

Require **forecast categories** (commit / best / upside) with evidence rules—same discipline as global sales ops.

## Cost structure and investments

- Separate **run rate** from **one-time** entry costs
- Tie headcount to **revenue milestones** (hire triggers, not vanity org)
- Cap **fixed cost** as % of revenue by maturity stage
- Investment cases need **ROI or strategic must-do** rationale

For detailed models, pair with `cfo-advisor` or `financial-analyst` if present in environment.

## QBR cadence and pack structure

Standard **60–90 minute** QBR agenda:

1. Results vs plan (revenue, margin, KPIs)
2. Market and competitive update
3. Initiative status (green/yellow/red)
4. People and org (hiring, attrition, culture signals)
5. Risks and escalations
6. **Asks to HQ** (decisions, resources, roadmap)
7. Next quarter priorities (max 5)

Slide discipline: **one message per slide**; appendix for detail.

## Variance management

For each material variance vs plan:

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Metric | What moved |
| Driver | Root cause (volume, price, mix, cost, FX) |
| Persistent? | One-time vs structural |
| Action | Owner, date |
| Ask | What HQ must change |

Avoid blame narratives; focus on **decisions** and **resources**.

## Handoffs

- Enterprise-wide capital allocation → `enterprise-strategist`
- Tax, treasury, transfer pricing → `cfo-advisor`
- Global sales comp and quota policy → sales leadership / `cro-advisor` if available
- Workforce planning philosophy → `chro-advisor`
