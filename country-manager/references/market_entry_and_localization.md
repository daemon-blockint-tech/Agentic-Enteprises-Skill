## Entry thesis

Before tactics, articulate a **one-page country thesis**:

- **Why this market now** (growth, regulation, competition, strategic account pull)
- **Who buys** (segments, use cases, willingness to pay)
- **How we win** (differentiation vs local and global competitors)
- **What must be true** (assumptions on regulation, product fit, CAC, sales cycle)
- **Investment and payback** horizon (link to country P&L—see `in_country_pnl_and_business_planning.md`)

Pair with `intl-expansion` when the user has not yet chosen markets; this reference assumes **country is selected** and execution is the task.

## Entry modes

| Mode | When to use | CM responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| Direct subsidiary | Control, long-term commitment | Entity setup, hiring, P&L |
| Partnership / distributor | Speed, regulatory constraint | Partner selection, enablement, audits |
| JV | Local capability required | Governance, board cadence, KPIs |
| Digital-first | Low touch, product-led | Payments, support hours, compliance |
| Acquisition | Fast share | Integration plan with corp dev; 100-day priorities |

Document **exit options** and **minimum scale** to justify fixed cost of local entity.

## Localization dimensions

Prioritize by revenue and risk impact—not everything at once.

| Dimension | Examples | Typical owner (matrix) |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Language, workflows, integrations | Product + CM market reqs |
| Pricing & packaging | Currency, tax display, tiers | CM + finance |
| Go-to-market | Messaging, channels, events | CM + marketing |
| Sales | Local references, RFP norms | CM + sales leadership |
| Support | Hours, language, SLAs | CM + support ops |
| Payments & billing | Methods, invoicing, refunds | Finance + product |
| Legal & privacy | Notices, DPA, data residency | Legal + `compliance-engineer` |
| HR & culture | Holidays, benefits narrative | `chro-advisor` + CM |

Use a **localization backlog** ranked by ROI and regulatory must-haves; tie each item to a metric (conversion, churn, sales cycle, ticket volume).

## Phased entry playbook

**Phase 0 — Validate (0–3 months)**

- Pilot customers or design partners; LOIs where possible
- Regulatory path confirmed with counsel (licenses, data, employment)
- Competitive win/loss patterns from comparable markets

**Phase 1 — Launch (3–9 months)**

- Minimum org: CM + sales/CS leads as needed
- First revenue milestone and reference customers
- Partner signed (if mode requires) with enablement plan

**Phase 2 — Scale (9–24 months)**

- Expand headcount against hiring plan
- Deepen localization backlog from Phase 1 learnings
- QBR rhythm stable; variance management mature

Each phase ends with a **gate review**: continue, pivot, or pause (see kill criteria).

## Partners and channels at entry

- Define **ideal partner profile** (coverage, technical ability, brand fit, financial stability)
- Economic model: margin, MDF, exclusivity, termination
- **Enablement**: training, deal registration, co-marketing
- Audit rights and brand compliance for franchise/JV modes

Detailed partner governance continues in `regulatory_partnerships_and_stakeholders.md`.

## Risks and kill criteria

Track leading indicators:

| Risk | Early signal | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Regulatory block | License delay, enforcement inquiry | Pause spend; escalate legal |
| Product misfit | Low activation, support themes | Narrow segment; HQ roadmap ask |
| Unit economics | CAC payback > threshold | Shift channel; reduce fixed cost |
| Partner failure | Missed commits, quality | Replace or go direct |
| Talent | Key role unfilled > 90 days | Interim HQ cover; adjust plan |

**Kill or pause** when assumptions break and HQ will not fund remediation—document factually for portfolio review.

## Handoffs

- Market prioritization → `intl-expansion`
- Entity and tax structure → `cfo-advisor` + counsel
- Technical compliance implementation → `compliance-engineer`
- Executive narrative for HQ → `ceo-advisor` (when board-level)
