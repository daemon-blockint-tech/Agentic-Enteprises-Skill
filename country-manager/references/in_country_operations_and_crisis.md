## Operational cadence

Run the country as an **operating system**, not only a sales plan:

| Ritual | Frequency | Participants | Outputs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Country ops review | Weekly | LT | Blockers, KPI flash |
| Pipeline review | Weekly | Sales + CM | Forecast update |
| CS / quality review | Biweekly | CS, product liaison | Churn drivers |
| Partner review | Monthly | Channel lead | Partner scorecard |
| Risk review | Monthly | LT + legal/compliance | Risk register update |

Integrate **customer incidents**, **regulatory touchpoints**, and **HR signals** into one risk view for QBR.

## Competitive dynamics in-market

Maintain a living **competitive brief**:

- Share shifts and new entrants (local and global players)
- Pricing and packaging moves
- Win/loss themes from sales and partners
- Product gaps vs local requirements

Translate intel into **actions**: positioning, localization asks, partner bets, pricing within policy—not generic SWOT slides.

## Reputation and brand locally

- Align local campaigns to **global brand standards**
- Monitor social and media for emerging issues (comms-led)
- **Employee advocacy** and local executive visibility where appropriate
- Avoid country-only promises that global cannot support

Escalate reputational threats early—speed matters more than perfect information.

## Crisis types and severity

| Severity | Examples | CM role |
|---|---|---|
| S1 — Critical | Regulatory enforcement, data breach, violence at site, major customer harm | Incident commander locally; HQ war room |
| S2 — Major | Service outage affecting country, senior exec misconduct allegation, viral negative press | Lead coordination; daily HQ sync |
| S3 — Moderate | Single customer escalation, localized protest, minor compliance gap | Owner with functional lead |
| S4 — Low | Routine complaints | Delegate to ops |

Define **what triggers S1** in advance with legal and comms.

## Crisis response workflow

1. **Detect and classify** — assign severity; do not under-classify to avoid HQ attention
2. **Activate cell** — CM, legal, comms, relevant functional lead; secure channel
3. **Facts only** — separate confirmed facts from assumptions; timestamp updates
4. **Stakeholder map** — who must know (regulators, customers, employees, media)
5. **Action owners** — remediation, customer comms, technical fix, regulatory response
6. **HQ alignment** — regional president and global exec sponsor informed per playbook
7. **Stand down** — explicit criteria; transition to recovery mode

CM does **not** draft legal filings or privileged strategy—business coordination only.

## Communications escalation

| Audience | Usually owns message | CM role |
|---|---|---|
| Customers | CS / comms | Approve business facts; escalate |
| Employees | HR + comms | Visible leadership; town halls |
| Media | Comms | Spokesperson only if designated |
| Regulators | Legal | Brief business context |
| Partners | Channel + CM | Direct relationship continuity |

**One voice** policy—no improvised statements on legal or security incidents.

## Post-incident review

Within 2 weeks of stand-down:

- Timeline of decisions
- Root cause (technical, process, human, external)
- Control gaps and owners
- Update to **crisis playbook** and training
- Customer/partner retention actions

Share learnings with global ops (`coo-advisor`) and compliance where controls failed.

## Handoffs

- Security incident technical response → cybersecurity roles
- Audit and control remediation evidence → `compliance-engineer`
- Global crisis comms strategy → communication-lead / `ceo-advisor` for board
- Business continuity and DR → BCM roles if present
