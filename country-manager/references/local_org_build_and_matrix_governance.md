## Target org design principles

- **Minimum viable leadership** at entry; add roles when revenue or risk justifies
- **Clear P&L line** for customer-facing functions; **dotted line** to global centers of excellence
- Avoid duplicate layers (country + regional + global) without decision rights
- Document **span of control** and **succession** for each direct report

## Leadership team composition

Typical country leadership team (scale-dependent):

| Role | Primary accountability | Matrix to |
|---|---|---|
| Country Manager / MD | P&L, stakeholders, matrix | Regional president |
| Sales leader | Revenue, pipeline | Global sales |
| Customer success / support | Retention, NPS, SLAs | Global CS |
| Marketing lead | Demand, brand local | Global marketing |
| Finance controller (country) | Forecast, controls | Regional FP&A |
| Legal / compliance liaison | Contracts, licenses | Global legal |
| HRBP | Hiring, ER, culture | `chro-advisor` org |

For greenfield, CM may **wear multiple hats** temporarily—time-box with hiring plan.

## Hiring and onboarding

1. **Profile** — outcomes, language, network, regulatory fluency
2. **Scorecard** — align with `talent-acquisition` on structured interviews
3. **Comp** — within global bands; local market data via HR
4. **Onboarding** — HQ immersion, customer visits, compliance training
5. **90-day plan** — measurable outcomes per leader

CM owns **who** and **when**; recruiting ops owns **process**.

## Matrix RACI patterns

Use a single RACI per major process (example: **launch new SKU in country**):

| Activity | CM | Country sales | Global product | Global legal | Finance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Market requirement | A | C | R | I | I |
| Pricing in band | A | C | C | I | C |
| Regulatory clearance | I | I | C | R | I |
| GTM plan | A | R | C | I | C |
| Revenue forecast | A | R | I | I | C |

**R** = responsible, **A** = accountable, **C** = consulted, **I** = informed.

Escalate **A** conflicts** in writing with options—do not let matrix stall revenue.

## Governance forums

| Forum | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Country LT meeting | Weekly | Execution, blockers |
| Matrix staff (product/eng) | Biweekly | Roadmap, incidents |
| Regional president 1:1 | Monthly | Alignment, career |
| Global functional reviews | Quarterly | Standards, talent |
| QBR | Quarterly | Results, asks |

Publish **decision logs** for cross-border topics (pricing exceptions, partner approvals).

## Culture localization

- Translate **values** into local behaviors—do not dilute non-negotiables (ethics, safety, compliance)
- Adapt rituals (meetings, recognition, holidays) without creating **second-class** global policies
- Listen for **psychological safety** and speak-up channels
- Address **language** barriers in documentation and all-hands

Partner with `chro-advisor` on sensitive ER topics; never provide legal employment advice.

## Performance and accountability

- Country KPIs on LT scorecards aligned to P&L
- **Forced clarity** on matrix goals: split local vs global objectives where needed
- Underperformance: fact-based PIP with HR; avoid public blame in matrix forums
- Celebrate **local wins** that advance global strategy

## Handoffs

- Enterprise operating model design → `enterprise-strategist`, `coo-advisor`
- Recruiting operations → `talent-acquisition`
- Compensation philosophy → `chro-advisor`
- Global engineering delivery → `cto-advisor` or engineering roles
