## Regulatory coordination (not legal advice)

The CM **coordinates** regulatory work; **counsel** provides opinions.

| Workstream | CM role | Specialist |
|---|---|---|
| Entity formation / licenses | Sponsor timeline | Legal, corporate |
| Product/market authorization | Business case, data | Legal, regulatory affairs |
| Data protection / privacy | Business process reqs | Legal, DPO, `compliance-engineer` |
| Employment setup | Headcount plan | Legal, HR |
| Sanctions / export | Customer screening process | Legal, compliance |
| Sector regulators (finance, health, etc.) | Relationship calendar | Legal, compliance |

Maintain a **regulatory milestone tracker** with owners and dates—flag slips in QBR.

**Never** state legal conclusions; summarize status and open questions for counsel.

## Compliance interfaces

- Align country operations to **global policies** (security, privacy, AML if applicable)
- For technical control evidence and audits, engage `compliance-engineer` and `compliance-specialist` if in repo
- Local incidents with regulatory notification potential → escalate legal immediately
- Train country LT on **speak-up**, gifts, anti-bribery, and data handling

## Partnership strategy

Define partnership **thesis** by type:

| Type | Strategic intent | Success metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Reseller / distributor | Coverage, speed | Revenue, attach, satisfaction |
| SI / implementer | Delivery capacity | Project margin, references |
| Technology | Integration, co-sell | Pipeline, retention |
| Strategic alliance | Market access, brand | Joint wins, roadmap alignment |
| Local JV | Regulatory/capital | Board KPIs, audit results |

**Selection criteria**: capability, financial health, cultural fit, conflict risk, exit ease.

## Channel economics and governance

Document for each major partner:

- Commercial terms (margin, rebates, exclusivity, territories)
- **Deal registration** and conflict rules
- Enablement obligations (training, certifications)
- Marketing fund use and proof-of-performance
- **Termination** and data return
- Audit rights (financial and brand)

Review partner performance in **quarterly business reviews** with clear improvement or exit paths.

## Government and stakeholder relations

High-level **stakeholder map**:

| Stakeholder | Interest | Engagement owner | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regulators | Compliance, industry health | Counsel lead; CM relationship | As required |
| Legislators / policy | Industry impact | Government affairs (if any) | Ad hoc |
| Key accounts | Outcomes, advocacy | Sales / CM | Ongoing |
| Media | Narrative risk | Comms | As needed |
| Local workforce | Trust | CM + HR | Regular |
| Communities | License to operate | Comms / CSR | Program-based |

CM sets **tone and prioritization**; specialized functions execute (comms, legal, GA).

For crises touching government or media, see `in_country_operations_and_crisis.md`.

## Industry bodies and standards

- Membership in trade associations where **credibility** and **intel** justify cost
- Participate in standards working groups when product interoperability matters
- Capture **competitive and regulatory intel** for QBR—not lobbying without counsel

## Documentation and audit trail

Keep decision records for:

- Partner approvals and exceptions
- Pricing and discount outside policy
- Regulatory submissions and responses (factual status)
- Stakeholder meetings (attendees, topics—not privileged legal strategy)

Supports audit, litigation hold, and HQ governance.

## Handoffs

- Contract drafting and negotiation → commercial-counsel / legal
- Technical audit evidence → `compliance-engineer`
- Tax and intercompany → `cfo-advisor`
- Corporate development M&A → `enterprise-strategist` + transaction roles if present
