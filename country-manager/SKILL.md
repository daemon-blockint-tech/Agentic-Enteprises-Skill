# Country Manager

## When to Use

- Lead **market entry** or **scale-up** for a country or regional operating unit
- Build or refresh **country P&L**, business plan, and territory **KPIs / QBR** cadence
- **Localize** go-to-market, pricing, product packaging, support, and brand for in-market fit
- Stand up or reshape the **local leadership team** and **matrix** interfaces to global HQ
- Coordinate **regulatory and compliance** workstreams with local counsel and global functions (not legal advice)
- Design **partnerships, channels, and distributors** for the territory
- Manage **government and stakeholder relations** at executive level (not lobbying counsel)
- Respond to **in-country crisis**, reputation, or operational escalation
- Run **competitive intelligence** and positioning for the local market
- **Hire and onboard** country leadership; adapt **culture and ways of working** locally
- Prepare **matrix reporting** packages for regional/global leadership and board subsets

## When NOT to Use

- Global corporate strategy, portfolio, or board-level where-to-play only → `enterprise-strategist`, `ceo-advisor`
- International expansion market selection without in-country operating plan → `intl-expansion` (pair with this skill for execution)
- Immigration, work permits, or employment law opinions → legal counsel (not this skill)
- Tax structuring, transfer pricing, or treasury policy → `cfo-advisor`
- Product roadmap, backlog, or feature prioritization → `cpo-advisor` (or product roles in repo)
- Engineering delivery, architecture, or sprint execution → `cto-advisor` (or engineering roles in repo)
- Hands-on audit evidence, control mapping, or SOC 2 pipelines → `compliance-engineer`
- Consulting workshops and issue trees without country P&L ownership → `business-consultant`
- Recruiting process design without country GM accountability → `talent-acquisition` (supporting role)
