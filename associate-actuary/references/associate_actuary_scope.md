## Role definition

An **associate actuary** is a mid-level credentialed professional (e.g., **ASA**, **ASAI**) who applies actuarial judgment beyond analyst execution: leads technical workstreams, drafts methodology memos, performs peer review, and communicates with management and auditors—while operating within **defined signing and governance limits**.

This skill addresses **professional practice and work leadership**, not primary spreadsheet/triangle production.

## Associate vs analyst vs fellow

| Dimension | Actuarial analyst | Associate actuary | Fellow / chief / appointed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Credential | Often pre-associate or partial progress | ASA, ASAI, or equivalent associate | FSA, FIA, FIAI, appointed actuary titles |
| Primary output | Exhibits, triangles, tie-outs, model runs | Memos, method selection, peer review, workstream lead | Opinions, enterprise strategy, final sign-off |
| Judgment | Applies approved methods | Selects methods within policy; escalates material calls | Owns material assumptions and regulatory opinions |
| Signing | None | Limited (firm/jurisdiction dependent) | Statutory opinions, chief signatory roles |
| Stakeholders | Actuary reviewer | Management, audit, regulator prep (associate depth) | Board, regulator, external opinion |

When the user’s question is **only** “how do I build this triangle,” route to `actuarial-analyst`. When it is **opinion year or enterprise risk strategy**, route to `actuary`.

## Typical responsibilities

Associate actuaries commonly:

- **Lead** quarterly reserving or pricing cycles with analyst support
- **Author** methodology sections of actuarial memos (not always executive summary alone)
- **Peer-review** analyst or junior associate work per firm tiering
- **Present** technical findings to finance, risk, or audit (with fellow backup for material items)
- **Coordinate** data requests, model versions, and assumption application (changes escalate to `assumption-setting` / fellow)
- **Mentor** analysts on documentation, QA checklists, and reasonability tests
- **Maintain** professionalism: CE, conflicts, confidentiality

Associates typically **do not**:

- Issue **appointed actuary** statements or sole statutory opinions (jurisdiction-dependent)
- Set **enterprise assumption policy** without governance forum
- Replace **legal** or **compliance** interpretation of filing requirements

## Lines of business

Scope by LOB for realistic associate work:

| LOB | Associate focus | Escalate to fellow/chief |
|---|---|---|
| P&C | Method selection, tail/cat judgment, rate indication narrative | Material reserve opinion, cat program change |
| Life | Experience study conclusions, assumption updates in models | Product filing, embedded value sign-off |
| Health | Trend and seasonality judgment, segment credibility | Large group renewals with regulatory sensitivity |
| Annuity | Liability cash-flow review, scenario sets | Guarantee and hedge program decisions |
| Pension | Valuation methodology support, data quality | Funding policy and trustee-facing advice → `pension-retirement-funds` |
| Reinsurance | Treaty economics review, ceded triangles oversight | Commutation, commutation-style settlements at scale |

## Boundaries and handoffs

| Topic | Associate actuary | Handoff skill |
|---|---|---|
| Triangle builds and exhibit prep | Oversee; analysts execute | `actuarial-analyst` |
| Final reserve/pricing opinion | Support; rarely sole signatory | `actuary` |
| Client engagement / SOW | Participate; not engagement owner | `actuarial-consulting` |
| Assumption governance papers | Contribute analysis | `assumption-setting` |
| ALM and investment strategy | Supply liability assumptions | `asset-liability-management` |
| Control evidence automation | Coordinate on data lineage only | `compliance-engineer` |

Always document **what the associate did** vs **what requires fellow/appointed review** in the workpaper header or memo limitations section.
