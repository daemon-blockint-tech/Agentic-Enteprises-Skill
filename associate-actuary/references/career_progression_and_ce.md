## Progression path overview

Typical actuarial career arc (simplified):

```
Student / candidate → Actuarial analyst → Associate actuary (ASA/ASAI) → Fellow (FSA/FIAI/etc.) → Leadership (chief, appointed, specialty)
```

**Competency shifts:**

| Stage | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Analyst | Technical execution, accuracy, documentation |
| Associate | Method judgment, workstream leadership, peer review, stakeholder communication |
| Fellow | Material assumption ownership, opinions, enterprise issues, mentorship |
| Chief / appointed | Strategy, regulatory interface, organization risk appetite |

This skill sits in the **associate** band; route adjacent stages to sibling skills.

## Analyst to associate transition

Signals the user is ready for associate-level expectations:

- Completes **credential** milestones (ASA, ASAI, or equivalent)
- Leads **cycles** with minimal rework on judgment calls
- Writes **memos** understandable to another actuary
- Delivers **peer review** that improves work quality
- Manages **stakeholder** questions without over-promising sign-off

Development actions (framework):

- Own a **LOB slice** or product line end-to-end for one cycle
- Present to **audit or finance** with fellow backup
- Join **assumption governance** meetings as contributor → `assumption-setting`
- Study **standards of practice** applicable to jurisdiction

Avoid positioning exam completion alone as sufficient for **signing authority**—employer role matters.

## Associate to fellow transition

Fellowship paths (FSA, FIAI, etc.) add **depth exams**, **modules**, and **experience** per institute rules—overview only here.

Associate should prepare by:

- Owning **material judgments** with documented alternatives
- Leading **cross-functional** studies (pricing + reserving coordination with governance)
- Building **regulatory** and **board** communication skills under senior sponsorship
- Mentoring **analysts** and **candidates**

Route **fellowship exam tutoring** requests away from this skill unless user wants only high-level pathway map.

## Continuing education and professionalism

Associate credentials typically require **ongoing CE/CPD**:

| Element | Agent guidance |
|---|---|
| Cycle | Annual or biennial credit targets per membership rules |
| Content | Technical, professionalism, business—mix per institute |
| Tracking | Firm LMS or institute portal; retain certificates |
| Ethics | Often includes professionalism course or attestation |

Agents should:

- Remind users to verify **current credit rules** on official sites
- Not invent **course lists** unless user provides approved catalog
- Tie CE to **role needs** (e.g., IFRS 17 update, cyber risk, data science ethics)

**Professionalism** also includes:

- Attestation of **code of conduct** compliance
- Disclosure updates (conflicts, outside interests)
- Participation in **peer review** and **quality** programs

## Stakeholder depth by career stage

| Stakeholder | Analyst | Associate | Fellow / chief |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actuary reviewer | Primary | Escalates judgment | Owns opinion |
| Finance | Data support | Explains drivers | Strategy alignment |
| Audit | Schedules | Walkthroughs | Judgment defense |
| Regulator | Rare | Prep support | Primary spokesperson |
| Board | No | Rare, scripted | Regular |

Associates should **practice** communication upward while **respecting** authority limits in `signing_authority_and_review.md`.

## Cross-border careers (brief)

Professionals moving countries should plan:

- **Credential recognition** — partial exam credit; not automatic practice rights
- **Regulatory licensing** — separate from ASA/ASAI
- **Local standards** — ASOP vs IAS vs local actuarial standards
- **Employer role mapping** — title inflation risk (“associate” job title ≠ ASA)

Advise consultation with **target institute**, **immigration/employment counsel**, and **hiring actuary**—not agent speculation on visa or license outcomes.

For technical work in new LOB, pair with `property-casualty-insurance`, `life-health-insurance`, or `pension-retirement-funds` as line context.
