## Standards landscape

Actuarial work is governed by **professional standards of practice** (jurisdiction- and organization-specific, e.g., US ASOPs, IAA IAS, local institute guidance) and **employer model risk / actuarial governance** policies.

At **associate** depth, agents should:

- Align recommendations to **documented purpose** of the assignment (pricing vs reserving vs review)
- Require **workpapers** sufficient for another qualified actuary to evaluate conclusions
- Flag when **legal** or **regulatory** interpretation is needed—defer to counsel and senior actuary

Do not quote standard numbers verbatim unless the user supplies the text; summarize obligations and point to official sources.

## Code of conduct themes

Common themes across actuarial codes (illustrative, not exhaustive):

| Theme | Associate practice |
|---|---|
| Integrity | No misrepresentation of methods, data, or credentials |
| Competence | Work within training; escalate material judgments |
| Confidentiality | Protect insurer, client, and policyholder data |
| Conflicts | Disclose dual roles (pricing + reviewing same block) |
| Communication | Clear limitations; distinguish fact vs judgment |
| Compliance with law | Cooperate with lawful requirements; seek counsel when unclear |

Associates often **model** ethical behavior for analysts: proper attribution, no pressure to “plug” to management targets without disclosure.

## Confidentiality and data

Associates handle **sensitive** information: claim triangles, pricing algorithms, reinsurance terms, M&A data rooms.

Minimum practices:

- **Need-to-know** distribution for memos and exhibits
- **Redaction** in examples (synthetic data in agent outputs)
- **Secure storage** per firm policy; no personal cloud for workpapers
- **NDA / engagement letter** awareness in consulting contexts → `actuarial-consulting`

When users paste **real** confidential data, remind them of handling policies; prefer aggregated or masked exhibits in chat.

## Conflicts and independence

Watch for:

- **Pricing and reserving** on the same block without independent review
- **Consulting** on a model the associate built as employee
- **Vendor** relationships affecting model selection
- **Personal investments** in lines of business under review (disclose per policy)

Document conflicts in a short **memo to file** and adjust review chain (independent reviewer, fellow sign-off).

## Documentation and reliance

Associates authoring methodology memos should include:

1. **Purpose and scope** — decision supported, basis, date
2. **Data** — sources, cutoff, known limitations
3. **Methods** — selected approach and reasonable alternatives considered
4. **Results** — central estimate and material sensitivities
5. **Limitations** — data gaps, one-time events, model constraints
6. **Reliance** — third-party data, models, or non-actuary inputs

**Reliance language:** distinguish **information furnished by others** vs **actuarial conclusion**. Do not extend reliance to **legal** or **tax** outcomes.

## Associate-specific pitfalls

- Signing or emailing conclusions **above** authority tier to meet deadlines
- **Verbal** approvals without workpaper trail
- Letting **management preferred answer** override documented analysis without sensitivity disclosure
- **Under-documenting** peer review comments (“looks fine” in chat only)
- Confusing **credential** with **regulatory license to sign**

Route enterprise assumption changes to `assumption-setting`; statutory opinions to `actuary` and appointed actuary.
