# Associate Actuary

## When to Use

- Clarify **associate-level** responsibilities vs actuarial analyst vs fellow/chief actuary
- Explain **ASA**, **ASAI**, or analogous associate credential pathways and credit structures (overview)
- Apply **professional standards**, code of conduct, and confidentiality at associate depth
- Define **signing authority limits**, review tiers, and approval chains for actuarial work
- **Lead** reserving or pricing workstreams, methodology memos, and peer-review cycles
- Communicate with **management, auditors, or regulators** on actuarial topics (associate-appropriate depth)
- Frame **continuing education** and professionalism requirements (framework-level)
- Plan **career progression** from analyst → associate → fellow
- Summarize **cross-border credential recognition** concepts (non-exhaustive, not legal advice)

## When NOT to Use

- Day-to-day triangles, IBNR builds, pricing spreadsheets, and exhibit prep → `actuarial-analyst`
- Appointed actuary opinions, chief actuary strategy, enterprise capital, or final sign-off → `actuary`
- Client SOW, consulting engagement design, M&A DD framing → `actuarial-consulting`
- Enterprise assumption governance, assumption papers, and change control → `assumption-setting`
- P&C coverage, claims, and underwriting mechanics without credential/role lens → `property-casualty-insurance`
- Life/health product and benefit mechanics only → `life-health-insurance`
- Pension funding policy and trustee advice → `pension-retirement-funds`
- ALM strategy, duration matching, and investment policy → `asset-liability-management`
- SOC 2 / ISO control evidence without actuarial judgment → `compliance-engineer`
- **Exam prep**, study plans, or question banks as the primary deliverable (route to education resources if user only wants tutoring)

## Core Workflows

### 1. Role and credential context

Before advising on authority or deliverables:

1. **Credential** — ASA, ASAI, other associate-level designation; jurisdiction of practice
2. **Employer type** — Insurer, reinsurer, consultant, regulator-facing captive, pension administrator
3. **Assignment** — Lead technical workstream, peer review, memo authorship, stakeholder meeting (not sign-off)
4. **Governance tier** — Materiality, model tier, required reviewer (fellow, appointed actuary, EQ)
5. **Out of scope** — Flag items needing fellow/chief actuary, counsel, or appointed actuary

**See `references/associate_actuary_scope.md`.**

### 2. Credential pathways (overview)

1. Map **exam/credit structure** at headline level (SOA, IAI, mutual recognition—no tutoring)
2. Clarify **membership vs credential** and continuing obligations
3. Note **experience / professionalism** requirements where material
4. Brief **cross-border recognition** concepts when user works internationally
5. Escalate **jurisdiction-specific filing or signing** rules to qualified human actuary and counsel

**See `references/credential_pathways_asa_asai.md`.**

### 3. Professional standards and ethics

1. Align work to **actuarial standards of practice** and firm code (overview)
2. Document **conflicts**, **confidentiality**, and **data handling**
3. Separate **technical indication** from **implemented** business decision
4. Disclose **limitations** and reliance boundaries in memos
5. Route **legal interpretation** to `commercial-counsel`; **enterprise assumptions** to `assumption-setting`

**See `references/professional_standards_and_ethics.md`.**

### 4. Signing authority and review

1. Classify deliverable: **workpaper**, **methodology memo**, **indication**, **regulatory exhibit**, **opinion**
2. Map **who may sign or certify** vs who may **author** or **peer-review**
3. Define **review chain**: analyst → associate → fellow → appointed/chief
4. Log **EQ / independent review** when policy requires
5. Never imply **statutory sign-off** the user cannot provide

**See `references/signing_authority_and_review.md`.**

### 5. Leading actuarial workstreams

1. **Scope** reserving, pricing, or cross-functional study with clear decision and date
2. Assign **analyst tasks**; retain judgment on method selection and conclusions
3. Draft **methodology memo** structure: purpose, data, methods, sensitivities, limitations
4. Run **peer review** with documented comments and resolution
5. Package **stakeholder summary** separate from technical appendix

**See `references/leading_actuarial_workstreams.md`.**

### 6. Career, CE, and stakeholder communication

1. Outline **progression** analyst → associate → fellow and skill expectations
2. Frame **CE / professionalism** obligations (framework, not course catalog)
3. Prepare **management or audit** talking points at associate depth
4. Coordinate with `actuary` for **opinion-year** or **board-level** messages
5. Archive **versioned** memos and review sign-offs per model risk policy

**See `references/career_progression_and_ce.md`.**

## Associate deliverable checklist

Before circulating a draft externally or upward:

```
Associate review:
- [ ] Role and credential appropriate to deliverable type (no implied sign-off)
- [ ] Methods and data period documented; limitations stated
- [ ] Peer review completed per tiering policy; comments resolved or excepted
- [ ] Signing/review chain identified; fellow/appointed actuary engaged if required
- [ ] Metrics and definitions consistent across memo, exhibits, and deck
- [ ] Confidentiality and conflict disclosures on file where needed
- [ ] CE/professionalism obligations not conflated with technical conclusions
```

## Deliverable standards (associate)

| Deliverable | Minimum content |
|---|---|
| Methodology memo | Purpose, scope, methods, sensitivities, limitations, review log |
| Workstream plan | Roles, milestones, data cutoff, review gates, escalation path |
| Peer review record | Findings, severity, resolution, re-test if applicable |
| Stakeholder brief | Decision-oriented summary; technical backup indexed |
| Credential / career note | Pathway overview, gaps, next steps—no exam tutoring unless requested separately |

State **uncertainty**, **authority limits**, and **non-advice** boundaries. Do not present associate-level output as **appointed actuary sign-off**, **legal advice**, or **regulatory filing** without qualified human review.
