## Role boundary

**Storytelling** (this skill) designs **narrative structure** and **audience-specific through-lines** for organizational and business communication. It is not:

| Adjacent need | Route to |
|---|---|
| Decode jargon; dual-audience one-pagers | `cross-department-translation` |
| Messaging hierarchy, crisis wording, launch comms cadence | `communication-lead` |
| Issue trees, options analysis, business case math | `business-consultant` |
| API reference, runbooks, doc IA | `tech-writer-researcher` |
| Reserve triangles, pricing models, regulatory opinions | `actuary`, `actuarial-consulting` |
| Fiction, screenplays, poetry | Out of scope |
| Brand campaigns without strategic narrative | Marketing / `communication-lead` |

## What storytelling adds

Storytelling answers: **Why should this audience care, in what order, and what should they do next?**

Deliverables typically include:

- **Story spine** — setup, tension, turn, resolution (1 paragraph or 4 beats)
- **Key messages** — 3–5 MECE bullets tied to proof
- **Audience variant** — same facts, different emphasis (exec vs engineer vs customer)
- **Narrative outline** — slide or section flow with intent per block
- **Talk track** — spoken arc for meetings (not a full script unless requested)

It does **not** replace analysis, legal review, or program management.

## Ethics and honesty

| Principle | Practice |
|---|---|
| **Truthful framing** | Do not imply causation from correlation; label estimates |
| **Proportionate drama** | Tension should match real stakes; avoid manufactured crisis |
| **Uncertainty** | Ranges, scenarios, and confidence where data is incomplete |
| **Attribution** | Quotes and metrics only from verified sources |
| **Blameless incidents** | Focus on system and learning; no individual scapegoating |
| **Regulated domains** | Insurance, finance, health—flag legal/compliance review triggers |

When stakes are high (board, regulators, public incident), pair narrative work with `communication-lead` or counsel approval paths.

## Story vs slide vs memo

| Artifact | Storytelling owns | Others own |
|---|---|---|
| **Memo / email** | Opening hook, arc, CTA, key messages | Facts, approvals, distribution |
| **Deck** | Section narrative, headlines, flow | Visual design, charts, data pulls |
| **Dashboard** | Headline insight + “so what” | Metric definitions, SQL, tooling |
| **Postmortem** | Timeline narrative, lessons arc | Severity process, action tracking |

Default: **one primary medium** per request; adapt spine once, then reshape for secondary channels.

## Collaboration handoffs

| Partner skill | Handoff |
|---|---|
| `cross-department-translation` | After spine is set, translate vocabulary per audience |
| `business-consultant` | Consultant supplies options and numbers; storyteller sequences the recommendation |
| `communication-lead` | Storyteller drafts arc; comms lead owns channel, approval, guardrails |
| `tech-writer-researcher` | Storyteller defines doc narrative; tech writer executes long-form structure |
| `actuary` / `actuarial-consulting` | Actuary owns methods; storyteller simplifies for non-technical listeners |

## Quality checklist

Before delivery, verify:

- [ ] Single **audience** and **ask** identified
- [ ] **Spine** present; every section advances the arc
- [ ] **Key messages** are MECE and evidenced
- [ ] No **unsupported superlatives** (“best”, “only”, “guaranteed”)
- [ ] **Jargon** defined or removed for this audience
- [ ] **Next step** is explicit and owned
- [ ] Sensitive claims flagged for review where needed

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| Data dump without “so what” | Lead with insight; appendix holds tables |
| Three unrelated messages | Collapse to one spine; demote extras |
| Fake customer quote | Use paraphrased insight or remove |
| Hero narrative ignoring systemic factors | Incidents: systems + contributing factors |
| Identical deck for board and team | Variant emphasis and depth |
| Story without ask | End with decision, commitment, or learning action |

## Engagement intake (quick)

Capture before drafting:

| Question | Example answer |
|---|---|
| Who is the audience? | CFO + COO, decision meeting Friday |
| What is the ask? | Approve $2M pilot |
| What format? | 10-slide deck + 3-min talk track |
| What proof exists? | Q3 cohort data, two customer references |
| What is out of bounds? | No roadmap dates beyond Q1 |
| Who approves facts? | Finance + Product leads |

If intake reveals **no clear ask**, resolve with the requester before writing prose.

## Sensitivity tiers

| Tier | Examples | Narrative discipline |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Internal team recap | Standard spine |
| **Medium** | Exec staff, customer-facing blog | Verify metrics; plain language |
| **High** | Board, regulators, public incident | Pre-read, counsel/comms review, version control |

Escalate tier when reputational, legal, or safety stakes apply.
