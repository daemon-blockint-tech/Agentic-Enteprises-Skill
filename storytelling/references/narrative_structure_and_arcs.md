## Story spine (default)

Use for most internal and customer narratives:

```
SETUP     — Shared context the audience accepts
TENSION   — Problem, risk, gap, or opportunity (specific)
TURN      — Insight, decision, or intervention
RESOLUTION — Outcome, path forward, or honest open question
```

**Example (product launch):**

- Setup: Customers lose time reconciling exports manually.
- Tension: Error rate rises as volume grows; support tickets up 40%.
- Turn: Shipped automated reconciliation with audit trail.
- Resolution: Pilot customers cut close time by 2 days; GA next month with training plan.

## Classic arcs for business

| Arc | When to use | Shape |
|---|---|---|
| **Situation–Complication–Resolution (SCR)** | Exec updates, consulting slides | Status → what broke/changed → what we do |
| **Problem–Agitate–Solve** | Persuasive customer or internal pitch | Pain → cost of inaction → solution (use ethically) |
| **Before–After–Bridge** | Transformation, migrations | Old state → target state → how we cross |
| **Hero’s journey (light)** | Change management, culture stories | Call → struggle → mentor/tool → new normal |
| **In medias res** | Incidents, postmortems | Start at impact → rewind → forward fix |

Pick **one** arc per artifact; do not stack metaphors.

## Key messages from spine

Derive **3–5 key messages** (KM):

1. Map each KM to one spine beat (usually TENSION or TURN).
2. Attach **one proof point** per KM (metric, example, quote).
3. Check **MECE**: no overlap; together they support the ask.
4. Order for audience: execs often want **recommendation KM first** (pyramid).

**KM template:**

```
KM[n]: [Claim in plain language]
Proof: [Metric, example, or exhibit]
So what: [Link to ask]
```

## Tension without manipulation

Effective tension names a **real constraint**:

- Market, regulatory, or competitive shift
- Customer pain with evidence
- Technical debt or reliability trend
- Financial or capacity limit

Avoid:

- Vague fear (“we might fall behind”)
- Inflated deadlines without source
- Straw-man alternatives

For **data stories**, tension is often “metric moved; here is plausible driver set” not “disaster.”

## Openings and closings

**Openings (pick one):**

- **Headline first** — “We recommend X because Y.”
- **Concrete scene** — One sentence of customer or operator reality.
- **Contrast** — “Last quarter … This quarter …”

**Closings (pick one):**

- **Ask** — Decision, budget, or approval with owner and date.
- **Commitment** — What we will do by when.
- **Learning** — What we know now that we did not before (postmortems).

Never end on “questions?” without restating the ask.

## Parallel structure and repetition

Use **parallel bullets** for comparisons:

- Option A: cost, risk, time
- Option B: cost, risk, time

Use **anaphora** sparingly in talks (“We will measure. We will publish. We will iterate.”).

Limit **rule of three** to three items when listing priorities—more than three dilutes memory.

## Templates

### One-paragraph spine

```
[Audience] needs [outcome]. Today, [tension with proof]. We [turn/decision].
By [date], [resolution]. The ask is [single CTA].
```
