## Audience dimensions

Tune narratives on four axes:

| Axis | Questions |
|---|---|
| **Authority** | Can they approve, veto, or only inform? |
| **Literacy** | Domain expert, informed generalist, or novice? |
| **Stakes** | What do they lose if wrong? |
| **Orientation** | Care most about risk, cost, speed, quality, reputation? |

Document the profile in one line before drafting: e.g., “CFO, informed generalist, cares about cash and audit risk.”

## Executive register

**Goals:** Fast decision, clear trade-offs, minimal detail unless asked.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Lead with recommendation and ask | Long background before the point |
| Quantify impact (range OK) | Technical implementation digressions |
| Name risks and mitigations | Jargon without definition |
| One page or three minutes | Appendix material in the main thread |

**Structure:** Pyramid — answer → supporting KMs → appendix.

Pair with `business-consultant` when the pack is primarily analytical; storyteller owns **sequence and emphasis**.

## Board register

**Goals:** Fiduciary clarity, strategic choice, oversight—not operations.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Strategic framing and material risks | Day-to-day ticket metrics |
| Clear decisions requested | Speculative forward statements without caveats |
| Consistent narrative quarter to quarter | Surprise new topics without pre-read |
| Reference governance hooks | Blame narratives |

Align with `communication-lead` for approved external/board communications.

## Customer and partner register

**Goals:** Trust, clarity, empathy, truthful product claims.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Customer outcome language | Internal org chart or acronyms |
| Specific timelines only when committed | Roadmap promises without ship proof |
| Acknowledge impact during incidents | Speculation on root cause |
| Single primary CTA | Multiple competing asks |

Route **contractual** or **SLA** language to `commercial-counsel`; storyteller drafts narrative around approved facts.

## Team and practitioner register

**Goals:** Shared mental model, actionable detail, psychological safety.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Explain “why” and “why now” | Leadership platitudes only |
| Link to docs, tickets, owners | Vague “we’ll fix it” without owners |
| Invite questions with structured FAQ | Surprises on sensitive topics in large forums |

For **cross-functional** vocabulary alignment without full story arc, use `cross-department-translation`.

## Channel constraints

| Channel | Narrative shape |
|---|---|
| **Live talk (5–15 min)** | Spine + 3 KMs; repeat ask twice |
| **Memo / email** | Subject = ask; first paragraph = pyramid top |
| **Deck** | Headline = insight per slide; one idea per slide |
| **Slack / chat** | Bold ask first; link to detail |
| **Status page / incident** | Facts, impact, workaround, next update time |
| **Recording / async video** | Chapter markers matching spine beats |

## Variant workflow

When one story must serve multiple audiences:

1. Write **master spine** once (facts + proof only).
2. Derive **audience variants** by changing:
   - Opening (what they already believe)
   - Depth of proof (summary vs appendix)
   - Ask (approve vs inform vs execute)
3. Keep **facts identical** across variants; never different numbers.
4. Log **variant table**:

| Audience | Opening emphasis | Ask | Depth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exec | ROI / risk | Approve budget | High-level |
| Engineering | Technical debt | Staff project | Detailed |
| Customer | Impact | Use workaround | Minimal |
