## Pre-submission checklist

```
VEE submission readiness:
- [ ] Correct society (SOA / CAS / both) selected
- [ ] VEE area matches course content bucket
- [ ] Course on approved list OR syllabus pre-cleared
- [ ] Final grade meets minimum; pass/fail rules checked
- [ ] Credit hours and level (UG/grad) match policy
- [ ] Official transcript ordered; name matches society account
- [ ] Syllabus PDF is complete for exact term/instructor
- [ ] Submission form course codes match transcript
- [ ] Fee paid; confirmation number saved
- [ ] Society record checked after expected processing window
```

## Common denial patterns

| Pattern | Why it fails | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong bucket | Stats course submitted as Economics | Map learning objectives before enroll |
| Unlisted course | Assumed “equivalent to School X” | Use society search tools |
| Insufficient grade | Below minimum threshold | Retake or alternate approved course |
| Incomplete syllabus | Missing topics or credit hours | Use department standard syllabus file |
| In-progress grade | Submitted before term ends | Wait for final transcript |
| Expired approval | Course delisted after enrollment | Submit promptly; watch policy emails |
| Name mismatch | Transcript vs society account | Fix with registrar early |

## Course and institution mistakes

1. **“Actuarial” labeled course** that is seminar-only without VEE topics
2. **Business calculus** substituted for statistics VEE where society requires inference coverage
3. **Dual-listed** courses submitted under wrong department code
4. **Online section** not approved though in-person section is
5. **Bootcamp** certificates without society-listed university partnership

Agents: explain **why** the pattern is risky; refer to society admin for edge cases.

## Grade and timing mistakes

1. Taking **pass/fail** option when letter grade required
2. **W/D** withdrawal appearing on transcript after submission attempt
3. **Repeated** course below threshold without understanding which grade counts
4. Waiting years after graduation—**syllabus archives** harder to obtain
5. Submitting during **policy transition** without reading transition FAQ

## Documentation mistakes

1. Unofficial web transcript screenshot instead of **registrar** document
2. Syllabus from **wrong year** (instructor changed curriculum)
3. Missing **translation** or evaluation for foreign credits
4. Partial upload set—one of three areas documented only
5. Claiming VEE on **résumé** before society status updates

## International and transfer pitfalls

| Situation | Pitfall | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Foreign bachelor | Assuming US grad school course auto-maps | Early society inquiry + evaluations |
| US transfer student | Only final institution transcript sent | Chain of transfer credits documented |
| English not language of instruction | Untranslated syllabus | Certified translation |
| Quarter vs semester | Credit hour shortfall | Advisor calculation to society rules |

No immigration advice. Process only.

## Integration with pre-actuarial study

Coordinate VEE with `pre-actuarial-foundations` study plans:

1. **Economics VEE** supports business literacy for internships; light overlap with exam P
2. **Accounting & Finance VEE** reinforces FM intuition—schedule before or with FM prep
3. **Mathematical Statistics VEE** complements but may not replace P preparation

Avoid **duplicate effort** without credit benefit:

- Taking two stats courses when one satisfies VEE and exam prep needs
- Delaying FM because finance VEE course scheduled later—sequence intentionally

University **actuarial science core** often maps many VEE areas—audit degree audit sheet freshman year.

## Recovery playbook

If denied:

1. Read **denial reason** verbatim from society communication
2. Gather **corrective** syllabus or alternate course plan
3. Contact **department coordinator** for historical syllabus retrieval
4. Resubmit per society instructions—do not assume informal email overrides
5. If fundamental mismatch, enroll in **known approved** online option for that area only
6. Update personal timeline for ASA/ACAS and communicate to employers if needed

Escalate **credential and ethics** questions to `associate-actuary`; **work product** questions to `actuarial-analyst`.
