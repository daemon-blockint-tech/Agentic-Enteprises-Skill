## Purpose of this reference

Provides **process orientation** for submitting VEE credit to the **Society of Actuaries (SOA)** and **Casualty Actuarial Society (CAS)**. Portals, fees, and forms change—users must follow **live society instructions**.

This reference does **not** constitute official society communication.

## SOA submission (framework)

Typical elements (verify on SOA education / VEE pages):

1. **Candidate account** on SOA systems with correct legal name
2. **Course identification** via SOA-approved course search or syllabus submission pathway
3. **Upload** of official transcript and syllabus when prompted
4. **Fee** payment if applicable for processing or transcript evaluation
5. **Processing time** — plan weeks, not days, during peak academic seasons
6. **Outcome** — approved, denied with reason, or request for more documentation

### SOA-oriented planning tips

- Complete VEE **before** or **in parallel with** early preliminary exams to avoid bottlenecks near ASA application
- Keep **digital copies** of all syllabi by term
- If denied, use society **appeal/resubmit** pathway rather than assuming informal email rulings

## CAS submission (framework)

CAS maintains its own education requirements (verify current CAS education manual):

1. **Membership / candidate status** requirements for submission
2. **VEE or equivalent** rules may differ in labeling from SOA
3. **Approved course lists** and online options are CAS-specific
4. **Documentation** standards analogous to SOA—transcript plus syllabus when required

### CAS-oriented planning tips

- P&C-focused students still need **VEE-equivalent coverage** per CAS rules—not “skipped” because of exam focus
- Align **ACAS** timeline with both exam sittings and VEE completion early in career

## Timing relative to preliminary exams

| Strategy | Advantages | Risks |
|---|---|---|
| VEE first (undergrad) | Clears admin work early; uses university billing | Policy changes before submission |
| Exams first | Focus study on P/FM | Delayed transcript submission; syllabus lost |
| Parallel | Efficient degree years | Overload; lower grades trigger VEE denial |

**Grade quality** matters for VEE; do not sacrifice VEE course grades for exam cramming without plan B course.

Exam study depth → `pre-actuarial-foundations`, not this skill.

## Timing relative to ASA and ACAS

High-level milestones (society-specific detail on official sites):

### ASA (SOA associate)

- VEE is typically part of **preliminary education** requirements alongside exams and professionalism components
- Incomplete VEE may **block** ASA application even when exams are done
- Agents should advise users to pull **official SOA transcript of requirements** before claiming “ASA-ready”

### ACAS (CAS associate)

- Similarly, education components include **VEE-equivalent** completion per CAS rules
- Exam progress without VEE documentation may stall **membership advancement**

Route **associate credential strategy, ethics, signing** → `associate-actuary`.

## Dual-path candidates

If pursuing **both** SOA and CAS:

1. Maintain **two checklists** of approved courses per society
2. Prefer courses appearing on **both** lists when possible
3. Submit to **each** society; approval by one is not automatic for the other
4. Track **separate confirmation** on each record

## Status verification

Before résumé, job applications, or exam registration attestations:

1. Log into society system and view **education / VEE status**
2. Screenshot or save **approval date** per area
3. If status pending beyond published SLA, contact **VEE administration** with submission ID
4. Do not rely on university advisor verbal confirmation alone

Expired policies: if society **sunsets** a VEE framework, follow transition announcements—agents must not invent grandfather rules.
