## Role boundary

**Validation by Educational Experience (VEE)** support is **framework-level guidance** for earning actuarial education credit through approved university or society-listed coursework. It is **not**:

- A substitute for SOA/CAS administrative decisions on a specific transcript
- Professional actuarial work (`actuarial-analyst`)
- Credential signing or ethics depth (`associate-actuary`, `appointed-chief-actuary`)
- Full exam preparation for underlying subjects (`pre-actuarial-foundations` and advanced exam skills)

| In scope | Out of scope (route) |
|---|---|
| Explain VEE purpose and topic buckets | IBNR, pricing exhibits → `actuarial-analyst` |
| Course-planning heuristics and checklists | Deep ASTAM/ALTAM math → advanced actuarial math skills |
| SOA vs CAS process overview | ASA ethics, signing → `associate-actuary` |
| Pitfall patterns and documentation lists | Official approve/deny on a named course |

## What VEE is

VEE is a **society-administered mechanism** that grants credit toward preliminary education requirements when a candidate completes **approved coursework** demonstrating coverage of defined subject areas (traditionally including **Economics**, **Accounting & Finance**, and **Mathematical Statistics**—exact labels and splits are society-defined and may change).

Key properties:

- **Course-based credit**, not a proctored preliminary exam sitting for that topic
- **Administrative validation** against published course lists and syllabus standards
- **Society-specific** rules (SOA and CAS maintain separate processes and listings)
- **Policy-dated**—requirements valid at submission may differ from earlier catalog years

Always instruct users to confirm **current** requirements on official SOA and CAS VEE pages before enrolling or submitting.

## VEE vs preliminary exams

| Dimension | VEE | Preliminary exams (e.g., P, FM, SRM, etc.) |
|---|---|---|
| Proof mechanism | Transcript + syllabus; society course approval | Proctored exam pass |
| Content source | Academic or listed online course | Society exam syllabus |
| Typical timing | During or after undergraduate study | Self-study or university prep |
| Agent role | Planning and documentation checklists | Exam study → `pre-actuarial-foundations` |

VEE **does not replace** the full preliminary exam suite. Candidates still pass society exams for most preliminary requirements; VEE addresses **specific subject waivers** only where societies allow.

## VEE in the credential ladder

At a high level (jurisdiction and society variations apply):

```
Pre-actuarial study (courses, tools)
    ↓
VEE topics completed + documented
    ↓
Preliminary exams (non-VEE subjects)
    ↓
Associate credential progress (ASA / ACAS) + professionalism
    ↓
Fellowship and specialty tracks
```

- **VEE** usually sits **early**—often before or alongside first preliminary passes
- **ASA/ACAS** milestones combine exams, VEE, and experience/professionalism components per live society rules
- Route **associate-level strategy** to `associate-actuary` when the user’s question is authority, signing, or career governance—not course selection

## Learner profiles

| Profile | Typical VEE needs | Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Traditional B.S. actuarial science | Map degree courses to VEE buckets early | Syllabus archive, registrar transcripts |
| Economics/finance major switcher | Gap analysis vs three VEE areas | Approved electives, online supplements |
| International graduate | Translation, credit hour equivalence | Society guidance + institution liaison |
| Working professional (no actuarial degree) | Online listed courses, grade thresholds | Timing with exam study and work |

Clarify **SOA vs CAS** target early—approved course lists are not fully interchangeable.

## Ethics and limitations

- State guidance is **educational**, not an actuarial opinion or society ruling
- **Do not guarantee** VEE approval for a named course, MOOC, or employer reimbursement outcome
- **Do not reproduce** live exam items or confidential society correspondence
- Cite that **official SOA/CAS VEE administration** supersedes any summary in this repository
- Respect **academic integrity**—help candidates understand requirements, not misrepresent syllabi
