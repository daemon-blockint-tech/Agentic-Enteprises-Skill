## Approval models

Societies typically use one or more of:

1. **Pre-approved course registry** — Course code at institution already mapped to a VEE area
2. **Syllabus review** — Candidate or department submits syllabus for one-off approval
3. **Listed online programs** — Third-party or university extension packages pre-vetted

Approval is **binary per society per topic** at a point in time. “Similar” content at a peer institution is **not** automatically equivalent.

## University courses

### How courses get listed (high level)

- **Actuarial program coordinators** align electives with society learning objectives
- **Departments** submit syllabi during accreditation or periodic society updates
- **Course renumbering** may invalidate prior approvals until refreshed

### Candidate actions

1. Search society tools for **institution + course number** before registration
2. If missing, obtain **full syllabus** (learning outcomes, texts, weekly topics, grading)
3. Ask **department actuarial advisor** whether submission is in flight
4. Register for the **correct term** and credit level (undergraduate vs graduate rules per policy)

## Online and extension providers

Categories (non-exhaustive, not endorsements):

| Category | Examples (illustrative) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Society-linked university extensions | University online portals | Often appear on official lists |
| Exam prep vendors with VEE packages | ACTEX, TIA, similar | Verify society listing and edition |
| MOOCs | Variable | Many are **not** approved unless explicitly listed |

Agents should:

- Direct users to **official course IDs** on society sites
- Warn against blog posts listing **outdated** provider SKUs
- Separate **exam prep** subscriptions from **VEE credit** products

## Syllabus fit checklist

Use when a course is not pre-listed or user questions fit:

```
Syllabus review (candidate-side prep):
- [ ] Institution name, course number, credit hours, term
- [ ] Prerequisites stated
- [ ] Learning outcomes mapped to society VEE objectives (user maps manually)
- [ ] Topic schedule shows required breadth (e.g., macro + micro for Economics)
- [ ] Assessment mix (exams, projects) and proctoring level if relevant
- [ ] Textbook/edition cited
- [ ] Graduate vs undergraduate level matches policy
- [ ] Language of instruction and materials
```

**Mathematical Statistics** fit often fails when:

- Course is **too theoretical** (probability measure) without applied inference
- Course is **too applied** (spreadsheet-only) without required statistical inference topics
- **Business statistics** variant omits regression/ANOVA components societies require

**Accounting & Finance** fit often fails when:

- Only **managerial accounting** without financial statements depth
- Only **investments** without corporate finance core topics

## Grades, credits, and repeats

Common policy patterns (verify live rules):

- **Minimum grade** (e.g., B- or equivalent) on transcript
- **Minimum semester credits** per area; quarter-system conversion matters
- **Pass/fail** courses may be excluded unless society allows
- **Retakes** — highest grade vs last grade per institution transcript policy

Document **final grade posting** before submission; in-progress courses need term completion first.

## Transfer, AP, and dual enrollment

| Source | Typical friction | User action |
|---|---|---|
| Community college transfer | Course codes differ from 4-year list | Get both transcripts; confirm 4-year mapping |
| AP/IB credit | May not carry syllabus detail | Society-specific AP policies; often need higher-level course |
| Study abroad | Foreign institution | Translation, credit hour equivalence, early society inquiry |
| MOOC certificates | Usually insufficient alone | Use only if explicitly on society list |

International agents: stay at **process** level; do not interpret visa or credential equivalency law.

## Agent boundaries

- **May** help compare syllabus topics to published learning objectives at a checklist level
- **May not** state “this will be approved” for a custom syllabus
- **Must** recommend contacting **SOA/CAS VEE admin** and **registrar** for edge cases
- **Must** caution against enrolling in a course that **ends after** a policy sunset if society publishes deadlines
