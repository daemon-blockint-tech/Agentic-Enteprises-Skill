## Framework overview

Societies organize VEE around **subject areas** backed by learning objectives. A candidate earns credit per area by completing **approved** instruction that satisfies those objectives, typically evidenced by:

- Official transcript showing course and grade
- Syllabus or detailed course description when requested
- Society course-ID or institution match to published lists

Topic **names**, **splits**, and **required depth** change over time. Treat any static list in training materials as **illustrative** until verified on the society site.

## Traditional topic buckets

The following are the **commonly cited** North American VEE areas (verify live requirements):

### Economics

Typical coverage themes:

- Microeconomics: supply/demand, elasticity, consumer/producer surplus
- Macroeconomics: GDP, inflation, unemployment, fiscal and monetary policy basics
- International trade and exchange-rate concepts at introductory level

Often satisfied by a **two-course sequence** or equivalent combined coverage—society rules define minimum hours and grade.

### Accounting and Finance

Typical coverage themes:

- Financial accounting: statements, accruals, ratios
- Corporate finance: time value of money, capital budgeting, cost of capital intro
- May span **two courses** (accounting + finance) or society-approved combined offerings

Overlap with university **business core** is common; still require **listed or syllabus-approved** fit.

### Mathematical Statistics

Typical coverage themes:

- Probability review as prerequisite bridge
- Estimation, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing
- Regression and ANOVA at applied statistics level
- May require **calculus-based** statistics per society policy

Distinct from **exam P** depth in many programs—VEE statistics is often **applied stats for actuarial students**, not measure-theoretic probability.

## SOA vs CAS framing

| Consideration | SOA (illustrative) | CAS (illustrative) |
|---|---|---|
| Admin body | SOA VEE process and course search tools | CAS education structure (verify current VEE mechanism) |
| Course lists | SOA-approved university and online courses | CAS-published equivalents and rules |
| Submission | SOA candidate systems | CAS member/candidate pathways |

**Do not assume** one approved course satisfies both societies without checking each list. When users pursue **both** paths, plan **dual validation** early.

## Coverage vs preliminary exam syllabi

| VEE area | Related preliminary content | Skill for deep study |
|---|---|---|
| Economics | Less direct exam analog; business literacy | `pre-actuarial-foundations` (concepts) |
| Accounting & Finance | Overlaps FM-style interest and finance intuition | `pre-actuarial-foundations` |
| Mathematical Statistics | Overlaps P and later statistical models | `pre-actuarial-foundations`; ASTAM for advanced stats models |

Studying for **exam P** alone may **not** satisfy VEE Mathematical Statistics if society rules require a dedicated statistics course with approved syllabus. Conversely, passing **exam P** does not automatically award VEE credit.

## Policy change and version risk

Agents should flag:

- **Catalog year** of the course vs year of degree completion
- **Society policy effective dates** when requirements are updated
- **Grandfathering** provisions (if any)—only society communications are authoritative
- **Retired** online providers or course codes still showing on unofficial blogs

Recommended user action: download or save **current** learning-objective PDFs and approval tables at enrollment time.

## Study routing

| User need | Route |
|---|---|
| Learn economics for understanding | `pre-actuarial-foundations` + standard textbooks |
| Learn stats for VEE + exam P | Society-approved stats course + exam prep plan |
| After VEE, advanced loss models | `advanced-short-term-actuarial-mathematics` |
| After preliminary exams, life contingencies | `advanced-long-term-actuarial-mathematics` |
| Credential milestones and ethics | `associate-actuary` |

VEE skill focuses on **credit mechanics**; foundational skills focus on **mastery**.
