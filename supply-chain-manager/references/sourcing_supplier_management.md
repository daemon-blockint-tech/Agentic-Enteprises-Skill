## RFQ process

1. **Scope** — specs, volumes, regions, Incoterms, payment terms (commercial input)
2. **Supplier list** — incumbents + challengers; conflict checks
3. **Bid package** — drawings, SLA targets, data requirements, timeline
4. **Q&A** — single channel; log amendments
5. **Evaluation** — scored matrix; site visit or audit if needed
6. **Recommendation** — award split, risks, transition plan

## Qualification

Minimum gates:

- Financial stability (credit check or statements)
- Quality system (ISO or equivalent where relevant)
- Capacity and lead-time credibility
- **Security/compliance** — route to `information-security-engineer` for data access
- References and pilot lot for critical parts

Maintain **approved supplier list** with category owner.

## Award criteria

Typical weighted scorecard:

| Factor | Weight example |
|---|---|
| Total landed cost | 30–40% |
| Delivery performance history | 25% |
| Quality / technical fit | 25% |
| Risk and geographic diversity | 10–15% |
| Sustainability / ESG | 5–10% (if mandated) |

Document **why not** lowest bidder alone.

## Supplier management

- **QBR** — scorecard, open CAPAs, roadmap, cost-down ideas
- **Escalation path** — operational → exec sponsor
- **Development** — joint improvement, VMI, consignment (if used)
- **Exit** — second source ramp before termination

Legal executes MSA/SOW — `commercial-counsel`.
