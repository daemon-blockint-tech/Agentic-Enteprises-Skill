## Risk categories

| Risk | Examples |
|---|---|
| Concentration | Single factory, single region |
| Geopolitical | Tariffs, export controls, conflict |
| Financial | Supplier distress |
| Quality | Recall, systematic defect |
| Logistics | Port strike, carrier capacity |
| Cyber | Supplier breach affecting shipment data |

## Assessment

Maintain **risk register**:

- SKU / supplier / region
- Likelihood and impact (1–5)
- Detection lead time
- Mitigation owner and status

Review quarterly; escalate **red** items to leadership.

## Mitigations

| Lever | Notes |
|---|---|
| Dual source | Qualify second before crisis |
| Safety stock | Costs cash; size to P95 LT + demand error |
| Near-shoring | Lead time vs unit cost trade-off |
| Design flexibility | Alternate approved parts |
| Contract terms | Force majeure, allocation, LTA volume |

## Continuity planning

For **tier-1** suppliers:

- Contact tree and escalation
- Minimum inventory or WIP at alternate site
- **War room** trigger (e.g. 2-week disruption)
- Communication plan for sales and customers

Regulatory export controls — involve legal/compliance early; no circumvention advice.

DC-specific delivery risk → coordinate with `senior-data-center-capacity-delivery-manager`.
